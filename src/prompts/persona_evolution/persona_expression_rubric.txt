You are evaluating how strongly a patient's persona traits were *expressed* in a single ED consultation. This is the dependent variable for our persona-evolution experiments — we are NOT scoring clinical quality, we are quantifying how visibly the patient acted out each personality / interpersonal trait.

For each axis below, rate 0-10 based ONLY on the patient's utterances in the dialog:

AXES (rate 0-10 each, higher = stronger expression of that trait)
1. distrust — questioning the doctor's competence, demanding evidence, refusing reassurances, suspicious tone.
2. pleasing — minimizing symptoms, downplaying severity, telling the doctor what they want to hear, "I'm fine".
3. impatience — irritation at slow questioning, abrupt answers, complaints about waiting / process.
4. overanxiousness — excessive worry, catastrophizing, naming feared diagnoses, requesting excessive workup.
5. verbosity — long, tangential answers, oversharing unrelated history, going off-topic.
6. guardedness — withholding information unless directly pressed, short non-answers, evasiveness.
7. resignation — low energy, "I don't know", "it doesn't matter", giving up.
8. directness — stating symptoms bluntly without softening, willing to volunteer relevant details.

OUTPUT — return ONLY a JSON object (no fences, no commentary):
